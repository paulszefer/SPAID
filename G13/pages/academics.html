What is CST?

Computer Systems Technology (CST) is a highly-sought after program offered by BCIT. CST is a two-year diploma program that fuses computer science with practical experience in software development. You’ll learn software engineering and programming from industry professionals, and gain experience working on real projects, from concept to deployment. In your third term in CST, you get to choose your preferred option -- a specialized area of study -- and take courses corresponding to that option.

Program Info

In CST, you’ll be introduced to core skills and theory used in software development. You’ll also be required to work as a part of a team to design and build an application. Second year is when you pick what specialty you want to go into. Currently there are eight different options to choose from. The number of courses is reduced; however, the level of work is increased. During second year, you’ll also be engaged with real-world industry projects. In CST, students are grouped into sets of about 25 students, and you stay with the same set for the first two terms. In terms 3 and 4, depending on what option you choose, you’ll be put into a new set.


Career Opportunities

The CST diploma is a distinguished credential that is highly-regarded by many employers. Graduates encompass the well-rounded skills needed for a career analyzing, designing, and developing software solutions. The tech industry is currently booming as every business is looking to get ahead by moving their business into high-tech infrastructure. In British Columbia, there are 9,700 tech companies and about 86,800 jobs and growing.


Courses

There are four terms in the CST program. For the first year, there are seven courses per term. The course content is moderately difficult, but the volume of work is immense.
