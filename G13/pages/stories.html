Bryce Watson

Current Company and Title: eBay Inc / Software Engineer MTS2 (Member of Technical Staff)
When did you graduate CST? 2013
Which CST option did you choose? Enterprise Development. May have been called something else, the one that focused on Java EE, JSB, etc.
What did you after you graduated CST? After graduating from the diploma I worked at the company I did my final project for, HyperWallet.
What is the most valuable skill you learned from CST? How to learn new skills under pressure. Confidence that anything can be learned with enough effort.
How did BCIT help you get your first job? Our final project was with HyperWallet. This gave me some key contacts that helped me get a job there, after a lot of perseverance.
What are some interesting things that you do during your job? • Solve problems on large, inter-connected systems, which mix together old and new technology.
• Build and maintain shared UI libraries which are used by multiple teams.
• Work with marketing, product, QA, and others to ensure our products are the best they can be.
• Experiment with new technologies such as React.js, Redux, Express.js, Node.js etc.
What did you do during CST to help you prepare for a job? I searched job posting and applied to everything that interested me, regardless of the experience requirements.
What type of job do you recommend to CST students? The best thing that ever happened to me was to start working at eBay in the U.S. I would recommend exploring jobs abroad once you have a few years under your belt, there is a very large demand down here and the pay is generally much higher than in Canada. Note: This would require a degree to satisfy VISA requirements.
What is your best interview tip? Focus your interview prep on actual practical knowledge, in addition to the usual questions. Being able to answer the usual algorithm stuff is good, but it’s even better when you can casually talk about implementation details of a relevant technology.
Tell us about a mistake you made during an interview or on the job: I once interviewed for a Senior Javascript position back when I really only knew Java. It was embarrassing and I regretted letting the recruiter talk me into it. I guess this isn’t “funny”, but maybe a good tip anyways.
What is a funny or unfortunate circumstance you encountered during your time in CST?: Many funny memories of inopportune youtube videos playing during Labs. Inside jokes, etc.

Current Students

Jessica Shao

Year / Semester: First semester
An intersting fact about you: I have a twin sister!
What was your expectation coming into CST? A lot of hard work and studying.
Is it(CST) as expected? I'm not as swamped as I expected I would be, but otherwise, pretty much. Yes.
What is your goal after achieving CST? I plan to spend another two years in the Game Design program.
What is your favorite CST subject so far? Well, COMM is the easiest for me, so that's enjoyable. But otherwise, COMP 1510 Java.
One interesting thing you have learned during CST? I've learned so many interesting things that it's hard to pick one.
Roughly how many hours do you spend a week doing CST? Not including the amount of time spent in classes, roughly 30 hours, I'd estimate.
How do you relieve stress from CST? Sleep and eat LOL Also sometimes I'll play some games or draw, but I get more stressed when I start thinking about how much time I'm wasting, so I'm currently looking for some less time-consuming stress relievers.
Where is your favorite hangout at BCIT with your fellow CST students? Honestly, as long as there is a place to sit and potentially some food, I'm good anywhere. I guess the Great Hall in between classes is where I end up the most, but the EhPod! is also a good place to get work done and spend time with my classmates.
Would you recommend CST to a perspective student? Yes! I love the cohort system and the hands-on portions of the program. When I think that graduating this program raises my possibility of finding a good job, it really motivates me and I think it's a good path for prospective students.
What should they be prepared for? Especially in COMP 1510 and COMP1536, reading the textbook and looking at online resources is more helpful than the lectures and powerpoint slides. The professors can provide good input though, if you talk to them directly.
What is your 2 biggest tips to perspective students? • Budget your time wisely to avoid getting overly stressed and swamped. Work builds up quickly.
•Maintain a good relationship with your classmates and professors. They are very good resources when you get stuck.
Finally, any suggestion to improve CST? Not that I can think of right now. When there are two one hour labs though, I'd really prefer it if it was just one two hour lab.
